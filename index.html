What is Carbon Footprint?

A carbon footprint represents the total greenhouse gas emissions (primarily carbon dioxide and methane) caused directly and indirectly by our individual lifestyles, travel, heating, diets, and waste disposal. Tracking it helps target high-impact reduction points.

The Global Target

To keep global warming under the 1.5°C threshold agreed in the Paris Accord, the global average footprint per person needs to drop from around 4.7 tons per year to under 2.0 tons per year by 2050.
